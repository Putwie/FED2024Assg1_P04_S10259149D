About Stray Kids:
Stray Kids is a South Korean boy band that was formed by “JYP Entertainment” through the 2017 reality television show. They debuted on March 2018. The band consists of eight members: Bang Chan, Lee Know, Changbin, Hyunjin, Han, Felix and Jeongin. Due to personal reason, Woojin (9th member of group) left the band in October 2019 Stray Kids primarily self-produce its recordings. The main production team of the group is called 3Racha, consisting of Bang Chan, Changbin and Han. As for the other members, they frequently participate in the songwriting process. In the year 2020, Stray Kid's first studio album called "Go Live" became the band's first platinum-certified album by the Korean Music Content Association (KMCA). On top of this, in that same year, Stray Kids made their Japanese debut with the compilation album called "SKZ2020" which was released through the Epic Records Japan. Stray Kids' second studio album called "Noeasy", became their first album to sell one million copies in 2021. Following their 2022 agreement with Republic Records for United States (U.S) promotion, the band released their third studio album, 5-Star (2023), as well as their extended play "Oddinary" and "Maxident" (2022), Rock-Star (2023), and Ate (2024). Their first five-charted albums made them the first band to debut at the top of the Billboard 200. These five records peaked at number one on the US Billboard 200 and made their way into the United Kingdom (UK) Albums Chart. Stray Kids not only was the third group in Korea to reach five-million sales for the album "5-Star", but they also received the certification from the KMCA for the "5-Star" album.
